Ratha Yatra

The Festival of Chariots of Lord Jagannath

What is Ratha Yatra?

Ratha Yatra, meaning "Festival of the Chariots", is one of the most famous spiritual and cultural festivals of India. It originates from the holy city of Jagannath Puri in Odisha, where thousands of devotees gather each year to celebrate the journey of Lord Jagannath, Lord Baladeva, and Lady Subhadra.

During the festival, the beautifully decorated wooden chariots of the deities are pulled by devotees through the streets of Puri. This joyful procession represents the Lord coming out of the temple to bless everyone, regardless of background or social position.

A Festival of Unity and Devotion

Ratha Yatra brings together people from all walks of life in a spirit of devotion, music, dancing, and celebration. Similar festivals are now celebrated around the world, sharing the message of spiritual harmony and compassion.

The Chariots

Each year, three magnificent chariots are constructed for Lord Jagannath, Lord Baladeva, and Subhadra. The towering wooden chariots are decorated with colourful fabrics and artistic designs before being pulled through the streets by thousands of devotees.

Experience Ratha Yatra

Modern Ratha Yatra festivals are held in many cities worldwide, allowing people everywhere to participate in this ancient tradition through chanting, cultural performances, vegetarian feasts, and spiritual celebrations.
